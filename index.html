🛡️ Safe Practices Checklist

Never pay any amount before verifying the company independently — legitimate internships don't charge candidates.

Search the company on Google, LinkedIn, and Glassdoor — look for real employees, reviews, and an address.

Insist on getting the offer and any terms in writing on an official company email or letterhead.

Talk to your college placement cell, a senior, or a trusted mentor before you commit to anything.

If something feels rushed or off, slow down. Real opportunities can wait a day or two for you to verify.

About InternSure

What this tool is — and isn't

Honesty matters more than confidence here. Here's exactly what you're getting.

This is not an official verification

InternSure does not contact companies, check government records, or confirm whether any business is registered or legitimate. It only analyzes the details you provide and compares them to patterns commonly seen in internship scams.

Think of it as a second opinion that helps you ask the right questions — not a stamp of approval or rejection on any company.

For real verification, also try

  • Your college placement or training & placement cell
  • The MCA portal, for a company's official registration status
  • LinkedIn, to check employee profiles and company history
  • Seniors or alumni who may have interned there before
