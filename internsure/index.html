Common Internship Scams

Legitimate companies pay you; you never pay them. If an agency demands upfront money for certificates, background screening, or specific mandatory software kits, walk away.

Fraudsters present fake interview stages to gather your physical signature, banking setup, PAN card/SSN information before extending a physical legally binding corporate contract.

Safety Tips for Students

  • Always check LinkedIns "Employees" list to ensure the HR representative actually works at the named company.
  • cross-reference domain senders. If the website is company.com, emails from @gmail.com are a major red flag.
  • Request a video call verification if the recruitment cycle feels unusually short (e.g. hired within 2 hours via text).
